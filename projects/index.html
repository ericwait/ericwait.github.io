High-impact technical projects from research to production

Building Systems That Make a Difference

From open-source GPU libraries used globally to production cancer diagnostics systems, my work spans the full lifecycle from research innovation to deployed solutions. Each project represents a commitment to combining technical excellence with real-world impact.

My Project Philosophy

Start Early, Stay Organized: MATLAB Utilities demonstrates over a decade of sustained, incremental improvement—building foundations before you need them. The best time to organize is when you have 10 functions, not 1,000.

Build What Lasts: Hydra Image Processor — first commit in 2013, still actively used and improved today (12+ years). The best optimization is one people actually use. Technical depth matters, but so does thoughtful design that serves users’ real workflows.

Human-Centered Design: Direct 5D Viewer leverages human visual perception (motion detection, depth sensitivity, pattern recognition) to turn terabytes of data into scientific insight. Technology should amplify human capability, not replace it.

Reverse-Thinking Methodology: Janelia Research Campus work demonstrates starting with what success looks like and working backward—preventing wasted effort on impressive but uninformative data. Published framework in Journal of Cell Science (2020).

Product Vision to Reality: Elephas Biosciences shows leading cross-functional teams from “what if?” questions to production clinical tools. 90%+ reduction in analysis times, multi-site reproducibility, FDA-aligned systems—disciplined execution that transforms curiosity into deployed solutions.

Art Meets Engineering: Home Media AI applies professional photography judgment to ML systems that curate 200K+ personal images. The intersection of artistic training and algorithmic implementation—translating “what makes a great photo?” into code that scales.

Home Media AI

Quick Takeaways

Problem: Finding “great photos” in 200K+ personal collections—combining objective technical metrics (sharpness, exposure) with subjective aesthetic judgment

Solution: Open-source ML system using computer vision, YOLO object detection, and hierarchical classification to surface best images

Philosophy: The intersection of art and engineering—translating professional photography judgment into algorithmic rules that scale

Key Insight: ML should multiply human capability, not replace it—review top 2,000 candidates instead of 20,000 images

Technical Approach: Python/SQLAlchemy, MariaDB, OpenCV/Pillow, YOLO for people/pet detection, scikit-learn for quality models

Direct 5D Viewer

Quick Takeaways

Problem: 5D microscopy data (x, y, z, channels, time) hard to understand—traditional projections lose depth information and create false conclusions

Solution: GPU-accelerated interactive visualization leveraging human visual perception (motion detection, depth sensitivity, pattern recognition)

Impact: Used by 170+ scientists at HHMI Janelia, enabled discoveries in Nature and Nature Communications, prevented algorithmic errors through visual validation

Philosophy: Human-centered design—technology should amplify human capability (visual perception) rather than replace it

Projects

Terabyte-Scale Biological Imaging at HHMI Janelia

Quick Takeaways

Challenge: Scientists acquiring terabytes of impressive but uninformative data—state-of-the-art microscopes without hypothesis-driven design

Solution: “Reverse-thinking” experimental design framework—start with what success looks like, work backward to determine requirements

Impact: 170+ international scientists annually, publications in Nature (2017, 2021), Nature Communications, framework published in Journal of Cell Science (2020)

Philosophy: Not all quantitative data are informative—prevent wasted effort by ensuring experiments can test the hypothesis

Key Insight: Don’t start with available tools—start with what an informative answer looks like, then work backward

Projects

Hydra Image Processor

Quick Takeaways

Problem: Biological microscopy analysis too slow (days per dataset with CPU-based methods)

Solution: GPU-accelerated library with accessible Python/MATLAB wrappers—leverage GPUs without writing CUDA

Impact: 100x speedups, global adoption by research institutions, published in Bioinformatics (2019)

Philosophy: The best optimization is one people actually use—accessible interfaces matter as much as raw performance

Status: 12+ years continuous development (first commit August 2013), still actively improved today
