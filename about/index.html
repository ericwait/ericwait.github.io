How I Solve Problems: Reverse-Engineering Solutions

I don’t start with available tools or technologies—I start by defining what success looks like, then work backward to determine the minimum path to achieve it.

This “reverse-thinking” methodology prevents teams from being distracted by cutting-edge capabilities that don’t serve the actual goal. The key principle: design is about exclusion, not inclusion. The best solutions carefully exclude unnecessary complexity while focusing resources on what actually matters.

Core approach:

  1. Define what success looks like (specific, measurable outcomes)
  2. Translate ambiguity into quantifiable metrics
  3. Work backward to determine minimum requirements
  4. Exclude everything that doesn’t directly serve the goal

This framework shaped work across research, consulting, and product development:

  • Pioneered hypothesis-driven experimental design for 170+ international scientists
  • Directed cancer diagnostics platforms achieving 90%+ reduction in analysis time
  • Co-founded Winter Wait Consulting LLC, applying this methodology to HPC optimization
  • Optimized Fortune 500 transportation networks by eliminating unnecessary computations

How I Lead: Translation and Empathetic Communication

A rare combination: Deep technical expertise paired with exceptional people skills.

My biggest strength isn’t just GPU programming or ML architecture—it’s bridging the gap between technical teams and product/project management.

The Translation Problem

Most organizations struggle with a fundamental communication barrier:

  • Product managers speak in user stories, market needs, and business objectives
  • Developers speak in architectures, APIs, and technical constraints
  • Lost in translation: Misaligned priorities, wasted effort, frustrated teams

What I Bring

Tailored Communication: I speak both languages fluently and translate seamlessly between them. Product managers get clarity on what’s technically feasible and why. Developers understand the business context and user impact of their work.

Empathetic Vernacular: Communication isn’t just about words—it’s about understanding motivations, concerns, and working styles. I adapt my approach to what resonates with each person and team, creating psychological safety that enables honest dialogue.

Increased Efficiency: When teams understand each other, development accelerates. Fewer misaligned features, less rework, faster iteration cycles, and higher morale.

Code Quality Catalyst: Beyond communication, I inspire teams to make their code more understandable, optimized, and production-ready. Not through mandates, but through empathetic mentorship that shows developers why clean code matters and how to achieve it sustainably.

Rare in Engineering: This combination—deep technical chops + empathetic people skills—is uncommon. Most strong engineers lack interpersonal finesse. Most great communicators lack technical depth. The intersection is where I operate.

This skill stems from 21 years of military service coordinating across intelligence, operations, logistics, and communications—groups with different priorities and languages. I learned to bridge gaps while keeping everyone aligned on the mission.

Technical Philosophy: Domain-Informed ML

I believe the best optimization is often the computation you don’t have to do.

By incorporating physical constraints and domain knowledge into ML systems, I build solutions that are more efficient and interpretable than purely data-driven approaches. This stems from my background in optics, signal processing, and real-world deployment—understanding that successful systems must respect both computational and physical limitations.

Domain-informed optimization isn’t just faster—it’s more interpretable, more trustworthy, and more aligned with how the world actually works.

Building What Lasts

  • 12+ years continuous development (first commit August 2013)

  • Still actively used because I prioritized accessible interfaces over raw performance alone
