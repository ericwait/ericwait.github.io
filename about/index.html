Bridging Physics and Machine Learning

I’m a Principal Data Scientist with over a decade of experience architecting GPU-accelerated machine learning systems that process terabyte-scale data in real-time. My unique approach combines domain knowledge with modern AI to create interpretable, efficient solutions that respect the physics of the problem space.

From Photography to Physics-Informed AI

My journey spans visual arts, military service, and computational science - experiences that taught me to see patterns others miss. At Elephas Biosciences, I led development of multi-modal ML systems that reduced inference latency by 60% through custom CUDA implementations. Previously at HHMI’s Janelia Research Campus, I built GPU-accelerated algorithms processing hundreds of terabytes of imaging data, achieving 10x speedups while maintaining scientific rigor. My work in Fortune 500 consulting optimized transportation networks, cutting computational runtime from days to hours through physics-informed optimization.

My Technical Philosophy

I believe the best optimization is often the computation you don’t have to do. By encoding physical constraints and domain knowledge directly into neural architectures, I build AI systems that require 80% less training data while generalizing better than purely data-driven approaches. This philosophy stems from my background in optics, signal processing, and real-world deployment constraints - understanding that successful ML systems must respect both computational and physical limitations.

Full Life Journey

I was born in the 80’s in a mid-size town in Wisconsin. I feel like my start was quite unassuming. My family had a heavy work ethic and would unwind in true Wisconsin fashion at the end of each week. I spent many of my earlier days playing on or around the Chippewa river. This is where I learned to waterski and enjoy the outdoors. I worked hard through my high school years to the point where I dropped some of my extra curricular activities. Hard work and the meager wealth that would bring was always the emphasis from my parents. I wanted more though. I found that joining the Air National Guard and attending college was a good compromise. The military would show that I could work hard and college would help me learn something that could not be had through experience alone.
