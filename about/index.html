How I Think: Reverse-Engineering Solutions

My problem-solving approach comes from years advising scientists at HHMI Janelia Research Campus, where I developed a philosophy I call “reverse-thinking”: start with what success looks like, then work backward to determine what you need to get there.

This methodology prevents building capabilities that don’t serve the ultimate goal. I don’t start with available tools or technologies - I start by defining what an informative answer would look like, translating ambiguity into measurable metrics, then determining the minimum path to achieve it.

Key principle: Design is about exclusion, not inclusion. The best solutions carefully exclude unnecessary complexity while focusing resources on what actually matters.

This approach shaped my technical contributions:

  • At Janelia, I pioneered hypothesis-driven experimental design, advising 170+ international scientists on translating vague biological questions into rigorous quantitative experiments - contributing to publications in Nature, Nature Communications, and Journal of Cell Science
  • In Fortune 500 consulting, I optimized transportation networks by selecting mathematical approaches that fundamentally reduced the problem space, eliminating unnecessary computations entirely and cutting runtime from days to hours
  • At Elephas Biosciences, I directed cross-functional teams to build cancer diagnostics platforms by defining success metrics first (90% reduction in analysis time, multi-site reproducibility), then architecting systems to achieve them

My Technical Philosophy

I believe the best optimization is often the computation you don’t have to do.

By incorporating physical constraints and domain knowledge into ML systems, I build solutions that are more efficient and interpretable than purely data-driven approaches. This stems from my background in optics, signal processing, and real-world deployment - understanding that successful systems must respect both computational and physical limitations.

Domain-informed optimization isn’t just faster - it’s more interpretable, more trustworthy, and more aligned with how the world actually works. Understanding the physics of your problem space lets you eliminate unnecessary computation and focus resources where they matter.
